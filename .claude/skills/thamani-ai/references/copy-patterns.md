## Insight Notifications

**Pattern:** `[Observation]. [Implication or action].`

Keep observations factual. The second sentence should either explain why it matters or
invite the user to explore further — never lecture.

### Examples

```
Your Food spending rose 15% this week. Tap to see the breakdown.
Transport costs dropped by KES 800. Your commute change is paying off.
You received KES 12,500 more than you spent this month. That's your biggest surplus since January.
3 of your top 5 categories decreased this week. Your patterns are shifting.
Weekend spending was 40% of your weekly total. See your day-by-day view.
```

### Anti-patterns

```
BAD: "You spent too much on Food this week."           → judgmental
BAD: "Alert: Food spending increased."                  → alarming, uses "alert"
BAD: "Your data shows a 15% increase in Food category." → cold, uses "data"
GOOD: "Food spending rose 15% this week. Here's what changed."
```

## Streak & Achievement Copy

**Pattern:** `[Achievement]! [What it unlocks or means].`

Celebrations should feel earned but not over-the-top. Use exclamation marks sparingly
(one per notification max).

### Streak Milestones

```
3-day streak! You're building a habit.
7-day streak! Your Weekly Reflection is ready.
14-day streak! Consistency looks good on you.
30-day streak! You've earned Master of Clarity.
60-day streak! Two months of financial mindfulness.
100-day streak! You're in rare company.
365-day streak! A full year of clarity. Incredible.
```

### Shield Achievements

```
Wood Shield earned! You've started protecting your spending.
Bronze Shield unlocked! Your limits are holding strong.
Silver Shield achieved! Financial discipline in action.
Gold Shield! You've mastered your spending boundaries.
```

### Streak Recovery (broken streak)

```
Your streak reset. Every new day is a fresh start.
Starting fresh today. Your insights are still here.
Day 1 again — and that's perfectly fine. Let's go.
```

## Budget & Shield Copy

**Pattern:** `[Status]. [Context without judgment].`

Budget language should inform, never shame. Frame limits as shields the user chose to
create — protective, not restrictive.

### Approaching Limit

```
You're at 75% of your Dining limit. 5 days left this week.
Transport shield at 80%. KES 400 remaining until Monday.
Entertainment is at 90%. You decide what happens next.
```

### At Limit

```
You've reached your Dining limit for the week. Your shield held.
Transport budget fully used. Reset happens Monday.
```

### Under Budget

```
Entertainment shield is holding strong. KES 1,200 remaining.
You're 30% under your Food limit this week. Nicely done.
All shields green this week. You're in full control.
```

## Onboarding Flow

Tone: Excited, simple, welcoming. Each screen should have one clear idea.

### Welcome Sequence

```
Screen 1: "Your money tells a story. Let's read it together."
Screen 2: "Thamani reads your M-PESA messages to reveal your spending patterns."
Screen 3: "Everything stays on your device. Your money, your business."
Screen 4: "Check in daily to build your Awareness Streak."
CTA: "Build Clarity" → [Grant SMS permission]
```

### Permission Request

```
"To understand your spending, Thamani needs to read your M-PESA messages.
We never upload, share, or store your messages anywhere but your phone."
Button: "Allow Access"
Secondary: "How does this work?"
```

### First Insight

```
"We found [X] M-PESA transactions from the last 30 days.
Here's your first look at your money story."
```

## Error & Empty States

**Pattern:** `[What happened]. [What to do].`

Errors should be calm and solution-oriented. Never use the word "error" — describe the
situation in plain language.

### Common Errors

```
We couldn't read your latest messages. Tap to retry.
Something went wrong loading your insights. Pull down to refresh.
Can't connect right now. Your data is safe — we'll try again shortly.
This page didn't load properly. Tap to give it another go.
```

### Empty States

```
No transactions yet: "Once you have M-PESA messages, your insights will appear here."
No streaks: "Check in tomorrow to start your Awareness Streak."
No budgets: "Set your first spending limit to activate a shield."
No insights this week: "Not enough transactions this week for a pattern. Check back soon."
```

### Offline State

```
"You're offline, but your insights are still here. Some features need a connection to update."
```

## Marketing & Social

Tone: Confident, aspirational, relatable. Lead with "Build" hooks from the tagline.

### Social Media Templates

```

"Stop guessing. Start seeing. Build Clarity."
"KES by KES, pattern by pattern. Build Thamani."

Medium (Instagram caption):
"Every M-PESA message is a piece of your financial story.
Thamani turns those messages into clarity — no manual entry,
no cloud uploads, just insight. Build Value."

Long (Blog intro / LinkedIn):
"Most people check their M-PESA balance and move on.
But what if you could see the patterns? The habits?
The story your money is telling you every single day?
That's what Thamani does. Build Future. Build Value. Build Clarity."
```

### App Store Description Hooks

```
"See where your M-PESA money really goes."
"Financial clarity without the spreadsheet."
"Your spending patterns, revealed — privately."
```

## Push Notification Library

All push notifications: max 2 sentences, curiosity-driven, always actionable.

### Daily Check-in

```
Morning: "Good morning! Your daily insight is waiting."
Evening: "How did today go? Tap to check in and keep your streak."
Missed: "You haven't checked in today. There's still time."
```

### Weekly Summary

```
"Your weekly reflection is ready. Tap to see your patterns."
"This week in numbers: [X] transactions across [Y] categories. See the story."
```

### Monthly Wrap

```
"Your [Month] wrap-up is here. See how your patterns evolved."
"[Month] is done. Here's what your money had to say."
```

### Re-engagement (lapsed user)

```
Day 3: "Your M-PESA messages are piling up. Want to see what they say?"
Day 7: "It's been a week. Your spending patterns are waiting."
Day 14: "We've been saving your insights. Come back when you're ready."
```
